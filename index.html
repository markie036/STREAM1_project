WHAT IS IT?

Mountain biking is the sport of riding bikes off road, normally over rough terrain. It differs to road cycling in that the bikes are more rigidly built, have thicker tyres and include suspension to help when riding over rough ground. The modern era of mountain biking took off in the late 70's and bikes have been constantly improved upon ever since. They range from hardtail to full suspension to downhill, with each bike specially designed to cope with each discipline. You will find a brief guide to each below.

The modern world of mountain biking is fast-paced, exciting and on the rise in terms of popularity. With world cup events, the olympics, as well as countless videos by amateurs posted online, exposure to the sport has increased exponentially. If there was ever a time to get involved, it is now. DISCIPLINES

Cross Country

The most popular and least extreme form of mountain biking, Cross Country usually involves riders traversing open fire roads to narrow single-tracks on a hardtail (single front suspension) or full suspension mountain bike. This discipline os good for beginners and will give a good introduction to mountain biking.

Enduro/All Mountain

This is a more technical and daring form of mountain biking which involves big drops and jump sections. Enduro riders usually opt for a full suspension mountain bike as it gives them better control and stability. Riders normally wear body armour and knee pads as well as enduro helmets (similar to motorbike helmets).

Downhill

This form of mountain biking is for the adrenaline junkie with nerves of steel. It involves racing down steep inclines at great speed over large jumps, rock formations and complex root sections. A downhill bike has a deep front suspension fork and extended central suspension to cope with the extreme punishment of this discipline.
